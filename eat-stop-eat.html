Frequently Asked Questions

Is intermittent fasting safe?

Yes, intermittent fasting is safe for most healthy adults. It's a natural pattern of eating that humans have followed for centuries. However, if you have any medical conditions or take medications, you should consult with your healthcare provider before starting.

Will I feel hungry all the time?

Actually, most people find that hunger decreases during fasting periods after the initial adjustment phase. Your body adapts to using stored fat for energy, which provides a steady source of fuel without blood sugar crashes that cause hunger.

Can I exercise while fasting?

Absolutely! Many people find that they have more energy for exercise during fasting periods. In fact, exercising in a fasted state can enhance fat burning. Just listen to your body and adjust intensity as needed.

Do I need to count calories on non-fasting days?

No, one of the biggest benefits of Eat Stop Eat is that you don't need to count calories. On non-fasting days, you simply eat normally without restrictions. The fasting periods create the calorie deficit needed for weight loss.
